Childhood Years

Hello, my name is Cyrus Songodanan, 21 years old and from Pasig City.1 I was born on December 18, 2003.2 I have so many childhood experiences, such as always playing outside the house and having happy friends who are still with me today.3 Being a child is one of the times I would like to go back to because there were no problems to think about, and all we did was play.4 We had strict parents, but they were also the reason why my childhood was happy.5 My parents always told me as a kid to be more sociable so I could make good friends, and because of that, I met many people, some of whom remain my friends to this day, while others are no longer in my life.6 I still remember how my parents would scold me as a kid because I wouldn’t take afternoon naps and would just keep playing outside.7 Aside from being scolded, my parents also had a sweet side, like the time they gave me my first guitar, and my older sister taught me how to play it.8 This sparked my love for playing the guitar, which I still enjoy today.9 It wasn’t just a guitar they gave me; they also gave me a bike.10 I remember always biking with my friends at a park near our house.11 I really enjoyed my childhood days because I got to try all kinds of sports and activities.12 I played basketball, volleyball, and badminton.13 These are things I still enjoy doing because I genuinely love these activities.14 Christmas is also one of the best parts of my childhood memories because it was the time I saved up a lot of money.15 Aside from money, I also received different toys and items from my godparents.16 I will never forget all my childhood experiences because they became the foundation of who I am today.17

Teenage Years

Of course, my teenage years were also wonderful.1 I explored many things about myself.2 When I was in grade 9, I started playing the guitar again, but I wasn’t very focused because I had difficulty playing the songs I liked.3 However, during the lockdown, things changed.4 I was so bored at home and had nothing to do except play online games and attend online classes, so I focused on playing the guitar.5 I tried playing a song every day until my fingers got used to handling the guitar.6 During that time, I developed a stronger love for singing and playing music.7 I also discovered that I could sing, even if just a little.8 When I was in grade 11 at UE Manila, I joined the Music Aficionado club, and thanks to them, I learned how to play music even though it was all online.9 I often joined their activities to expose my skills and gain the experience I needed to grow.10 One of the happiest moments before I joined this club was when they made me play a song I didn’t know.11 I spent a week learning it and tried my best to join the club.12 All the hard work was worth it because I made it into the music club.13 My teenage experience didn’t end there, though.14 Before I graduated, we had our prom, and I was one of the performers on stage with my friend.15 I became even more passionate about playing music as many of my fellow students sang along with us while we performed, and the feeling was amazing.16 With every performance, I learned to become more confident in myself and love the things I do.17 With the help of my friends and mentors in Music Aficionado, I became happier expressing myself through music.18 I can’t count how many songs I learned in this club, and with each song, I learned to trust my abilities.19 In the end, music became my inspiration to keep growing and dreaming, not just in this art but in my life as a whole.20

College Years

My college experiences are not that hard yet because I have many friends who help and support me through the challenges of college life.1 I know that college isn’t easy, and you need friends who will stand by you through the hard times and the happy ones.2 Right now, I have friends who never leave me even when we’re all struggling; we fight together for our future.3 My love for music still remains strong during college life.4 Since I started college, I joined the UE JAM SESSION, and that’s where I experienced performing on stage with people watching us.5 We have a band called 'Da Binsi,' and we have two vocalists, with me playing acoustic guitar.6 I didn’t just join a music club in college, but I also became a member of an organization called Comsso, and currently, I’m one of the officers.7 I’ve met many upperclassmen because of the clubs, and now they provide ideas and guidance for my upcoming college journey.8 They guide me in what I should do and what I shouldn’t.9 I feel blessed to be surrounded by people who care about my growth and success.10 I also try my best to share the knowledge I’ve gained with others, so we can all help each other.11 Despite the pressures of college life, these experiences make it all worthwhile.12 I know that the friendships I’ve built and the lessons I’ve learned will shape who I’ll become in the future.13 Sometimes, we have late-night talks about our dreams, which make me more motivated to push myself forward.14 Even though college can be overwhelming, having my friends and the right support system makes it manageable.15 Every new challenge becomes an opportunity to grow, and I’m excited for what lies ahead.16 I’m also starting to see the importance of balancing both my academic and personal passions.17 The skills I’ve gained in music, along with my academic pursuits, will help me build a future I’ve always dreamed of.18 In the end, college isn’t just about the degree; it’s about finding yourself, making lasting memories, and learning how to face life’s challenges with confidence.19
