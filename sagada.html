Sagada in the Philippines

 

The reason I want to visit the sagada is to see the hanging coffins and the beautiful mountains. Sagada is a small town in the Cordillera Mountains, within the Philippines' Mountain Province. It's known for its rich culture, stunning landscapes, and unique burial traditions. The town is surrounded by lush pine forests, scenic limestone caves, and terraced rice fields, making it a popular destination for nature lovers, hikers, and adventure seekers. Sagada is also home to the famous hanging coffins, a traditional burial practice of the indigenous Igorot people. These coffins are suspended on the sides of cliffs or in caves, symbolizing the deceased's ascent to the afterlife. The town's serene atmosphere, cool climate, and friendly locals make it an ideal place to relax, unwind, and immerse yourself in the beauty of nature.

Other places to visit in Philippines
• Northern mountains of Sagada - The northern mountains of Sagada are special for their breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique burial traditions. Home to the famous hanging coffins, lush pine forests, and scenic limestone caves, these mountains offer a peaceful retreat and an immersive experience in the indigenous Igorot way of life.
• Sagada and Banaue Rice Terraces - The Banaue Rice Terraces, often called the "Eighth Wonder of the World," showcase the Ifugao people's ancient ingenuity in sustainable farming. Carved into the mountains over 2,000 years ago, they remain a symbol of cultural heritage and resilience.
• Diplomat Hotel - The Diplomat Hotel in Baguio City is special for its haunting history and eerie atmosphere, making it a popular spot for thrill-seekers and history enthusiasts. Once a retreat house before being used as a hospital during World War II, it is now an abandoned ruin known for ghost stories and panoramic views of Baguio.
